Where we go

The Islands

Every island in the Galápagos has its own personality. We run tours on three of them — here's what makes each one worth the trip, and where to find the full list of tours for each.

Our Home Base

San Cristóbal

The capital of Galápagos — and where our office calls home. Sea lions nap right on the boardwalk, Kicker Rock (León Dormido) rises dramatically from the sea just offshore, and everything from day hikes to advanced diving departs a short walk from our door.

Highlands & Wildlife

Santa Cruz

The most populated island, home to the Charles Darwin Research Station and the misty highlands where giant tortoises roam free in the wild. From Las Grietas' turquoise canyon to advanced dive sites like Gordon Rocks, it has the widest range of tours in the archipelago.

Volcanoes & Coast

Isabela

The largest island in the Galápagos, shaped by six active volcanoes. Snorkel the lava arches of Los Túneles, hike the rim of Sierra Negra's massive caldera, or spot penguins and white-tip sharks resting in the Tintoreras lava channels.
